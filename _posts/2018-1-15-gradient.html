For now, LOOK AT MY NAILS WOW.

Isn't that trippy?

For all of you not in the nail art world, this is called a reciprocal gradient, because it reciprocates(?). Idk I didn't make up those words. For more on gradients, read Simply Nailogical's very confusing but practical gradient guide.

I started off with a Peel Off Base Coat.

Pro Tip: Don't do what I did. A peeloff basecoat is ~fine~ if you're not using nail vinyls or striping tape...but I was. So unless you want to be sad when your manicure is pulled off, use a normal base coat.

Why did I use one, you ask? I felt like challenging the nail Gods. But I did end up having to redo my pinky for my sins.

I used the black polish LBD and the white polish Linen from Live Love Polish. The black is opaque in one coat (gasp!). I would say the white is opaque in two coats.

Then I painted my nails white, I'm very creative. I put on a quick dry top coat after that to prevent the base coat from lifting up when I peel away the striping tape.

Next, I put on a layer of liquid latex to protect my skin from nail polish gradient disasters. More pro tips: Don't do this with a peeloff basecoat because the liquid latex might stick to the base coat. And it did for me. And I was sad. The end. *shakes fist at nail Gods*

Now for the fun part, the ~gradients~

I painted a sponge. I put that sponge on my nails. It looked like a gradient. I'm so happy T_T

Then I put on tiny pieces of striping tape on my nails. I don't usually use striping tape becase I have nail vinlys and stamping plates, but these let you have more creative freedom (or so I hear).

And then I make the reciprocal part happen. I put on the graduent again, but in the reverse order! Woah! I'm so innovative, I know. It took me about two to three coats to get this blended.

Then it's over. You're done. Nail art. It's magic.

This took me like two hours because nail art is hard. You can't rush ~art~ trust me, I've tried.
